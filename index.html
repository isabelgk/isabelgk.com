Simply Knit

Originally a Python script, Simply Knit is an Android app I am developing to help knitters create customized patterns based on their knitting styles. Given a stitch gauge and desired product dimensions, the app returns a personalized pattern for a no-guesswork knit product.

Source code
GoCD Sandbox

As an intern at edX, I used Docker containers to build a sandbox for the continuous deployment platform GoCD. Since changes to GoCD were made and stored as Python code (using the open source gomatic library), testing changes to the GoCD environment before deployment was highly desirable.

OneFlo

This project won first place in my chemical engineering project course at MIT. Using MATLAB and a DSLR camera, the OneFlo program could either use a grid or image recognition method to label cells in a transparent reactor and then determine kinetic, heat transfer, and reaction parameters.

Music

Piano was my first instrument at seven years old, and I started playing trumpet at nine before moving to French horn at ten. As an undergraduate, I am completing a second major in music. Check out my YouTube channel for more recordings.

I currently study horn with Eli Epstein at New England Conservatory as an MIT Emerson Fellow. I am an active member of the MIT Chamber Music Society, playing in woodwind quintet and brass quintet under Jean Rife as a coach. In high school, I played in a woodwind quintet coached by Barli Nugent, director of chamber music at Juilliard. In addition, I have played with the MIT Symphony Orchestra, New Jersey Youth Orchestra, NJ All-State Orchestra, NJ All-State Wind Ensemble, NJ Region I Orchestra, NJ Region I Wind Ensemble, North Jersey Area Wind Ensemble. I have played in Symphony Hall, Carnegie Hall, the Musikverein, and the MuTh.

In 2018, I was awarded the MIT Philip Loew Memorial prize for my work transcribing Guillaume Du Fay's Veni sante spiritus from the medieval manuscript Bologna, Museo Internazionale e Biblioteca della Musica di Bologna. I wrote my senior seminar paper on the symmetry of Bach's Goldberg Variations and am currently interested in applying computational music theory to more of Bach's corpus.
